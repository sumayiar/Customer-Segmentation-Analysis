# Executive Summary

- Analysis window: trailing 12 months ending 2026-03-26.
- Customers analyzed: 1,200.
- Orders analyzed: 13,001.
- Selected clustering configuration: KMeans with 5 clusters, validated against agglomerative clustering (ARI 0.66). This stayed within 0.010 silhouette points of the top statistical score while yielding a more actionable segment split.
- Highest-value segment: Growth Loyalists with 54.1% of revenue from 31.2% of customers.
- Highest-risk premium segment: High-Value At Risk with 20.9% of revenue and an average recency of 150 days.

## Retention Opportunities

- Growth Loyalists: Accelerate growth in a segment with 0.63 purchases per month. Recommended action: Nurture with cross-sell journeys, replenishment prompts, and category education.
- High-Value At Risk: Win back premium shoppers showing a 150-day average gap between purchases. Recommended action: Trigger churn-save journeys with personalized offers and category-specific reminders.
- Champions: Protect 15.0% of revenue coming from your most active customers before competitors do. Recommended action: Launch VIP early-access drops, concierge service, and referral incentives.
- Discount-Driven: Reduce margin pressure from a segment averaging 44.8% discount dependence. Recommended action: Shift from blanket discounts to bundles, loyalty points, and threshold-based promotions.
- Dormant: Contain spend on low-response audiences while testing efficient reactivation hooks. Recommended action: Use low-cost win-back campaigns, then suppress long-unresponsive customers from paid media.

## Strategic Readout

- Prioritize lifecycle marketing around high-value segments, then protect margin by moving discount-led customers toward bundles and loyalty benefits instead of blanket promotions.
- Use recency, spend, and discount intensity together in CRM scoring so retention budgets go to segments where revenue upside and churn risk overlap.
