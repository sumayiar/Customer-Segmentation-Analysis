# Project Story

## Objective

Build a portfolio-ready customer analytics project that segments shoppers by purchasing behavior, isolates the highest-value audiences, and turns those segments into practical retention and targeting recommendations.

## Approach

1. Generated a realistic 12-month retail transaction history for 1,200 customers.
2. Engineered behavioral features such as recency, order frequency, spend, discount intensity, and channel mix.
3. Evaluated cluster counts with KMeans, then selected 5 clusters because it stayed within 0.010 silhouette points of the best score while creating more actionable segments.
4. Translated clusters into business-facing segment names and retention actions.

## Key Findings

- Growth Loyalists: 31.2% of customers contribute 54.1% of revenue with an average order value of $139.
- High-Value At Risk: 13.8% of customers contribute 20.9% of revenue with an average order value of $170.
- Champions: 13.5% of customers contribute 15.0% of revenue with an average order value of $88.

## Retention Opportunities

- Growth Loyalists: Nurture with cross-sell journeys, replenishment prompts, and category education.
- High-Value At Risk: Trigger churn-save journeys with personalized offers and category-specific reminders.
- Champions: Launch VIP early-access drops, concierge service, and referral incentives.
- Discount-Driven: Shift from blanket discounts to bundles, loyalty points, and threshold-based promotions.
- Dormant: Use low-cost win-back campaigns, then suppress long-unresponsive customers from paid media.

## Business Strategy Impact

- Concentrate premium lifecycle messaging on high-value customers with strong spend but widening recency gaps.
- Improve margin discipline by replacing always-on discounts with loyalty mechanics for price-sensitive segments.
- Use the segment output in CRM, paid media suppression lists, and merchandising decisions to align spend with customer value.
